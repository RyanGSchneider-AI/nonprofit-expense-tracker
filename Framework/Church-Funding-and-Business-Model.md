## 3. Business Model

This is a nonprofit religious organization. It does not have a commercial business model. Value creation is mission-driven — spiritual community, pastoral care, outreach, and facilities stewardship for the congregation and broader community.

**How the organization sustains itself:**
The congregation funds operations through voluntary giving. The Finance Committee allocates those funds across operating expenses, staff compensation, denominational apportionments, building maintenance, and ministry programs. Surplus is held as reserve or directed to capital needs.

**Cost structure (major categories):**
- Staff compensation (Pastor full-time; Office Administrator full-time; Music Director and Organist part-time; Youth and Children/Family Leaders part-time)
- Facilities — utilities, maintenance, insurance, capital improvements
- Denominational apportionments — required UMC connectional giving
- Ministry programs — outreach, missions, youth, children/family, worship
- Administrative — supplies, software, communications

**The organization does not:**
- Distribute surplus to owners or members
- Operate commercial ventures as a primary revenue source
- Hold debt on behalf of members
